The Story of Razzou

The idea to launch an app first came to fruition in 2010. Dating apps at the time targeted gay men, yet I knew it was only a matter of time before mobile dating went mainstream.

Leaving my tiny hometown in Louisiana with little money but a solid business plan, I began raising capital to develop and launch my app. On my own, I raised $200,000 in capital to finance the launch of my dating app, named Razzou, which earned over 100,000 users worldwide.

Managing a Start-up

As CEO of an early-stage, start-up company, my responsibilities were far-reaching.

My Roles and Responsibilities Included:

  • Product Vision: Maintaining Razzou’s product vision of a world without missed connections, allowing users to meet potential matches by nearest proximity.
  • UX Design: I designed Razzou’s logo, website, and the app’s entire user interface, which consisted of over 100 different types of screens including login screens, chat screens, settings screens, and detailed user profiles. I even redesigned the user interface four separate times, ensuring users an exciting mobile dating experience!
  • Market Research: I conducted frequent analyses of leading competitors, studying their app interfaces, target markets, business models, and pricing, all while keeping up with rapid innovations in the smartphone market to maintain a competitive edge.
  • Day-to-Day Activities: My responsibilities included resolving tech issues as quickly and efficiently as possible, while remaining flexible and adaptable enough to overcome any unforeseen difficulties. When the app’s server failed, for example, thousands of profile photos were lost, and I had to figure out how to get them back—then make sure it couldn’t happen again!
  • Brand Management: Razzou targeted mobile daters between the ages of 25 and 34-years-old. Every icon, marketing image, color scheme, to even the name “Razzou” was made to best appeal this target audience.
  • Project Management: I managed the app’s numerous concurrent development projects, budgets, along with a small team of business partners, software engineers, and web developers.
  • Investor Management: My job required continuous reporting to company investors and the nurturing of key business relationships.
